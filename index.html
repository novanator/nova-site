About





Nicole Nova
Research Associate, Department of Biology, Duke University
D.D.S., M.Sc., B.Sc. in Dental Surgery, Karolinska Institute

Resume/CV

Interests: Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, Mathematical Modeling, Eco-Evolutionary Dynamics, Population Genetics, Phylogenetics and Conservation Genetics.



My current research focuses on the eco-evolutionary dynamics of pathogen-host co-evolution. In particular, I'm interested in how pathogens adapt to different host environments and what the implications are for the progression and spread of infectious diseases. I'm using theoretical approaches, such as mathematical modeling and population genetics, to elucidate the underlying mechanisms of biological systems.

I'm currently a Research Associate in the Department of Biology at Duke University, working in the Koelle Research Group. I'm working on mathematical modeling of the development of broadly neutralizing antibodies (BnAbs) in chronic HIV-infections. Studying the co-evolution between the potent BnAbs and HIV could potentially provide useful insights for developing effective HIV vaccines.

Prior to my position at Duke, I worked in the Michor Lab in the Department of Biostatistics and Computational Biology at Dana-Farber/Harvard Cancer Center (Dana-Farber Cancer Institute, Harvard Medical School and Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health). I was working on mathematical modeling of cancer development and progression using theories from evolutionary dynamics, population genetics and stochastic processes.

In 2012, I earned a Doctor of Dental Surgery (D.D.S.) and a M.Sc. degree from Karolinska Institute in Sweden. As an Erasmus Mundus scholar I also studied abroad at Queen Mary's School of Medicine and Dentistry in London, UK. I completed my Master's thesis in the Molecular Pain Lab in the Department of Physiology and Pharmacology and the Department of Oral Physiology at Karolinska Institute. My thesis was on the pathophysiology of chronic pain in rheumatoid arthritis studied in vivo using a mouse model (more information here).
































In addition, I completed a clinical internship in the Department of Head and Neck Surgery at the Medical University of Vienna in Austria. I became interested in the evolution of cancer on a cellular level and that led me to evolutionary medicine and eventually to ecology and evolutionary biology.

During my years of clinical experience and biomedical research, I saw an opportunity for emerging technologies to improve clinical outcomes. I became fascinated by mathematical biology and how one could build mathematical models used for in silico experiments (computer simulations) that could replace some time-consuming and expensive clinical trials. Furthermore, I also realized the value of a solid mathematical background for studying ecology and evolution in general. In order to build a quantitative foundation, in addition to my medical background, I also studied Electrical Engineering at the Royal Institute of Technology in Sweden (below is a video of a robot that I and a team of engineering students built using Segway technology).

Outreach

Research Science Institute (RSI)

RSI is a summer research program for high school students at Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and organized by the Center for Excellence in Education (CEE). As an RSI alumn, I have been involved in helping to run the program. I attended RSI as a student in 2007 - I was working with Prof. Jeremy Wolfe in the Visual Attention Lab in the Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences at Harvard Medical School and Brigham and Women's Hospital. I was investigating whether simple search tasks (i.e. looking for a green line among red lines) was correlated with searching for objects in the real world. In particular, searching for weapons in airport security (X-ray images of luggage). The work was later published in the Journal of Vision.



Research Academy for Young Scientists (Rays)

Rays is a summer research program for high school students, similar to Research Science Institute (RSI), but in Sweden. It was founded and run by a group of Swedish RSI alumni (including me). We organize mentorships at prestigious universities in Stockholm, such as Karolinska Institute, Stockholm University and the Royal Institute of Technology. After a summer-long internship in a research lab, the students present their projects in the Swedish Museum of Science and Technology.
